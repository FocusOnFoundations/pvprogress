The Portola Valley Town Council is seeking two volunteers to serve on the Town's Architectural and Site Control

Commission (ASCC). One of these term’s expires in December 2023 and the other expires in December 2024.

The ASCC consists of five members appointed by the Town Council, and meetings are held on the 2nd and 4th Mondays

of each month. The ASCC is charged with the review and approval of design review applications and site development

permits, including applications for new homes, second units, larger additions, second-story additions, and commercial

buildings. In addition, the ASCC provides comments on conditional use permits, subdivisions, variances, and other

matters referred by the Town Council, the Planning Commission, or Town Staff.

To apply, please submit a letter of interest to the Town Council to towncenter@portolavalley.net by 5:00 pm on January

27, 2023. The Town Council will conduct interviews and appoint new members at a scheduled meeting on The week of

January 30, 2023 This time is TBD to change, dependent on the number of interviews to be conducted. If you have

questions or would like more information, please contact the Town Clerk, Melissa Thurman

at towncenter@portolavalley.net.

Re: Planning Commission

To: Towncenter@portolavalley.net

After much deliberation and consulting with other residents about the ASCC and the Planning

Commissions, I’ve decided to volunteer to serve on the Town's Planning Commission (PC).

Portola Valley is facing important planning decisions over the next year and beyond that will shape how

the Town can develop in a manner that will respect our commitment to the preservation of open space

and the conservation of our natural environment. All decisions of the Planning Commission must be

made balancing our General Plan with the laws and regulations of the state of California.

Portola Valley exists in a stunningly beautiful landscape of mountains, hills, and steep ridges straddling

the geologically active San Andreas Fault, covered with oak forests and riparian zones along creeks and

riverbeds. It’s critical that all land use plans respect and preserve that uniqueness while also making it

possible for a wider variety of families and individuals to join Portola Valley.

There are several activities in my background that would be relevant for the Planning Commission.

I have a very strong analytical background with master’s degrees from the University of Pennsylvania

and INSEAD in France. This background enables me to easily grasp complex concepts, understand

detailed government regulations, and make logical decisions.

I have closely followed the massive efforts of the Ad-Hoc Housing Element Committee, Stanford Wedge

Redevelopment project, and the Safety Element and have frequently contributed multiple ideas for

meeting our housing goals. The efforts to refine the Housing Element as well as find solutions for

increasing affordable housing in our Town in a manner that respects and preserves our natural

environment will continue to be active efforts for several years to come as the Town grapples with

finding locations for affordable housing.

I’ve been on the Sustainability Committee for many years (and the Water Conservation Committee,

prior to that). Given the extreme and critical situation of climate change, it is vitally important that the

Town continue to encourage the adoption of green and sustainable building practices and be at the

forefront of those efforts. I routinely attend lectures, workshops, and green home tours on green

building, air sealing, energy efficiency, water conservation, and passive house building and have a strong

understanding of sustainable building practices.

In addition, I’ve recently been serving on the ADU Ambassadors Team where I’ve become very familiar

with the new regulations for ADUs and JADUs. As the Town seeks to find lower impact housing, ADUs

and JADUs represent a significant portion of our new Housing Element that can meet those

requirements with less of a visual impact on the Town as a whole.

Finally, I’m very knowledgeable about building plans, zoning regulations, permitting, and architectural

drawings as I have been involved in the past in designing my own remodel and general contracting a

major project at my own property.

Regards,

s/Rebecca Flynn

To the Town Council of Portola Valley,

I’m writing to once again express my interest in applying for the position of Commissioner on the

ASCC. I decided to write a new letter because the Town Council meeting of 1/11 was such an

impactful experience for me. This letter covers two topics—1) why I’m interested in serving on

the ASCC, and 2) a response to comments made about me at the 1/11 TC meeting.

When I was 27 years old and considering moving back to Portola Valley from Mendocino

County, I experienced a series of dreams about a place—about the natural environment, the

built environment, the trails that connect, and the people that inhabit; about building

community—socially and physically. The place was always exquisitely beautiful. I came to

understand—to my then surprise—that the place in my dreams was Portola Valley. I did not

know at that time how this place lived in me.

Now I know. This place was woven through me as the weft in the fabric of my being. The

formative impact this place has had on me has been profound and continues to reveal itself. I

am made from this place and for this place.

Nowadays I walk through this landscape with an awareness of layers of time and history. I feel

and contemplate the presence of all humans who live/d here—past, present, and future; the

landscape and changes to it—past, present, and future; and the built environment and

human-nature interaction—past, present, and future. I see that we are experiencing one

important moment in a timeless story of a magnificent place.

I feel grateful to have been formed by this place, and I would be deeply honored and committed

to the opportunity to help shape this place now, as part of that timeless story.

My work has been in carpentry, building, landscape, farming, land management, and community

organizing. The relationship of humans to place and community is paramount in my

consciousness. I’m passionate about design and the built environment, and how they interact

with the natural environment and human communities and the wellbeing of both.

I would like to clarify the comments made about me at the January 11th Town Council meeting,

regarding my capacity to fulfill responsibilities as a town volunteer. The comments were made

regarding my capacity to chair two committees, but obviously implicitly commented on my

capacity to serve on the ASCC.

Three people (and one of those people twice!) commented that I’m not capable of competently

fulfilling volunteer responsibilities because of my young family, and that I have been saying so

myself. These comments were a manipulation and stretching of the truth. The facts of the matter

are:

• In October of 2021 I asked to step down as chair of the Finance Committee because a

baby would be arriving in our family and I knew my capacity would be temporarily

reduced, as my wife and I made the decision to spend as much time as possible with our

children in the first year of Ruth’s life. No one else stepped up to chair. In 20/20

hindsight, I would have simply stepped down and someone would have had to step up,

but I didn’t have that perspective then.

• In March of 2022, a few weeks after the baby was born, Rita Comes emailed me about

the town audits. It took me a long time to respond and when I did I acknowledged the

circumstances of being a new parent.

• In October of 2022, I acknowledged that for the past year my bandwidth to perform the

duties of the chair had been reduced—as I had said would happen—but that I had now

returned to restored bandwidth and capacity.

• In January 2023, we are sleeping much better and have the support of a nanny to care

for our children. Everything changes.

These facts were distorted and manipulated by those who spoke of me at the January 11th

Town Council meeting. For example, one speaker said:

“As of December this person was saying on meetings that she didn’t have

enough time, due to her young family, which makes perfect sense, um, to catch

up with all the finance committee stuff.”

I reached out to this individual to substantiate their remarks. They were unable to do so,

because they weren’t factual. This is just one of various comments that simply weren’t factual.

I understand that members of the public have the right to say things that are not factual in public

comment, but I want to point out, for your record, that those public comments were erroneous

and manipulative and typify a pattern of misinformation that has grown in our town’s public

discourse in the past few years.

Best regards,

Lucy Neely
